Harriman State Park

Description:

At 47,527 acres, Harriman State Park is the second largest state park in New York State. Located in Rockland and Orange counties 30 miles north of New York City, it is a haven for hikers with over 200 miles of hiking trails.

    Trails you can find here:
  1. Silver Mine Lake Loop(easy)
  2. Timp Torne Blue R-D red trail Loop(moderate)
  3. Black Mountain Loop(hard)

South Mountain Reservation

Description:

South Mountain Reservation, covering 2,110 acres, is a nature reserve on the Rahway River that is part of the Essex County Park System in northeastern New Jersey, United States.

    Trails you can find here:
  1. Turtle Back Trail Loop(easy)
  2. Watchung Reservation History Trail(moderate)
  3. Lenape Trail: South Moutain Reservation (hard)

Richard W. Dekorte Park

Description:

Estuary park with walking paths plus an environmental center and observatory access on select days.

    Trails you can find here:
  1. Dekorte Park Meadowlands Trail(easy)

Bear Mountain State Park

Description:

Bear Mountain is one of the best-known peaks of New York's Hudson Highlands. Located partially in Orange County in the town of Highlands and partially in Rockland County in the town of Stony Point, it lends its name to the nearby Bear Mountain Bridge and Bear Mountain State Park that contains it.

    Trails you can find here:
  1. Perkins Memorial Tower via Appalachian Trail (easy)
  2. Bear mountain loop via twin forts(moderate)
  3. Dunderberg, Bald Rocks, Timp Loop (hard)

Mount Tammany

Description:

Mount Tammany is the southernmost peak of the Kittatinny Mountains in Knowlton Township, Warren County, New Jersey, United States. It is 1,526 feet tall, and forms the east side of the Delaware Water Gap. Across the Gap is Mount Minsi, on the Pennsylvania side of the river.

    Trails you can find here:
  1. Red Dot Trail (easy)
  2. Blue Dot Trail (moderate)
  3. Mount Tammany and Sunfish Pond loop (hard)
